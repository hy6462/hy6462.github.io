Research Essay


Focus of Research:

Social Anxiety can be described in a number of ways, most typically as extreme shyness. -Ranges from being helpful and adaptive (being very aware of their surroundings) to impairing (ruining the person’s ability to develop or maintain relationships) -Not to be confused with being just shy. Difference: Shy: is a personality trait. People who are shy do not associate their shyness with negative thoughts, and can normally function in their lives with their shyness. About 15 million North American adults have social anxiety disorder Typical age of onset: 13 years old 36 percent of people with social anxiety disorder report symptoms for 10 or more years before seeking help
