Design Proposal


Notes:

Idea #1:
Exhibition for raising awareness about social anxiety
corresponding app + brochure
forums for talking about social anxiety
tips for finding jobs, dating advice etc etc

Idea #2
Cafes where people can discuss their anxiety
an idea of “comfort”

Idea #3
Game for practising social situations
Inspired by therapy groups where people go over social situations in
real life, it helps them stumble and stutter less
Member site+sign up site+promotional site

Decision: Idea 3

Target Audience: People who have mild to moderate social anxiety.
The target audience for social anxiety is broad, since anyone could have it, no matter what their demographics are.
TEENAGERS (AGE 13-18)
 Social Anxiety occurs most commonly during teenage years. Unfortunately, they’re also the years in which they commonly have little knowledge about the topic – and ridicule those who have social anxiety, labeling them as names such as “geek” or “wallflower.”
YOUNG ADULTS (AGES 18-35)
 These young adults who live in Toronto, Ontario cover profiles of single and family households. This is a general target audience with a large range in income level, religious views, education background, hobbies and many other factors.
