UX/UI Design

Retail Demo

Inaccurate inventory has been a problem in retail for as long as we can remember.
So how can IoT (Internet of Things) be used to solve this problem? By digitizing inventory with technology such as RFID tags, each and every retail item could be tracked, reducing time spent looking at how many things are in stock, and where the item is.

(above illustrations) Six use cases where IoT can help retailers, illustrations made by me.

Smart Inventory Insights

I mocked up an extension of an existing demo, showcasing the benefits of RFID tags in retail stores. Imagine if each product was tagged with an RFID tag, allowing retailers to track each one. I focused on the Insights part of the app, allowing retailers to view reports on several areas of focus.

Fitting Room Usage Tracker

Think of the products that are brought in the fitting room by customers, linked to the number of sales. By being able to track how often a product goes to the changeroom vs. how many of it is sold, it gives the retailer opportunity to analyze if there are any flaws to the way the product is marketed.

Service Index

You could view by product or sales associate of how many times a product has been looked up and how many times an associate has been looking up products throughout the day. This enables retailers to track which product should be replenished more often. Although it's arguable, the sales associate report may encourage people to be more service oriented.
